Biography

Marina Satti (Greek: Μαρίνα Σάττι; born 26 December 1986) is a Greek singer, composer, music producer, and actress. Her music combines traditional Greek, Arab, and Balkan sounds with urban elements, rhythm, and contemporary production.

Born in Athens, Greece, Satti lived there until the age of six before being raised on the island of Crete in Heraklion. Her father was an Arab doctor-anesthesiologist from Sudan, and her mother is a chemical engineer from Heraklion with ancestry from the Greek population of Asia Minor.

Satti began piano lessons at five years old and later studied classical singing and jazz. She also pursued architecture for three years at the National Technical University of Athens and studied at drama schools. In 2012, she completed a three-year degree in jazz composition, contemporary writing, and production at the Berklee College of Music in the U.S.A.

Throughout her career, Satti has performed globally, collaborated with various artists, and founded "Fonés" (Voices), a female a cappella group, and "CHÓRES", a choir of 200 women, promoting Greek traditional songs. She has also contributed her voice to numerous cartoons, including Moana and The Little Mermaid for Greece. She represented Greece at the Eurovision Song Contest 2024 with her song "Zari".

Musical Style and Career Highlights

Satti's distinctive musical style seamlessly blends traditional Greek, Arabic, and Balkan sounds with contemporary urban elements, characterized by unique rhythms and production. Her diverse artistic endeavors include successful music releases, international tours, and extensive work in theater and voice acting.

Early in her career, she was a member of the EBU's European Jazz Orchestra and performed with the World Jazz Nonet at the John F. Kennedy Center in Washington, D.C. In 2011, she collaborated with Bobby McFerrin and the a cappella vocal group The Singing Tribe.

In 2016, Satti founded "Fonés" (Voices), a female a cappella group performing traditional polyphonic songs. She also established "CHÓRES", a choir of 200 women, dedicated to promoting Greek traditional songs through performances in archaeological sites. Her breakthrough came with singles like "Koupes" (2016) and "Mantissa" (2017), which gained widespread attention and millions of views.

Satti has performed in over 20 countries and collaborated with notable artists. She has also lent her voice to 24 cartoons, including the Greek versions of Moana (2016, 2024) and Ariel in The Little Mermaid (2023).

Eurovision Song Contest 2024

On October 24, 2023, Marina Satti was announced as Greece's representative for the Eurovision Song Contest 2024, held in Malmö, Sweden. Her entry was the song "Zari" (Greek: "Ζάρι", meaning "Dice"), which she co-wrote.

"Zari" fuses traditional Greek and Balkan music with urban vibes and pop/rap influences, sung primarily in Greek with some English phrases. The song aimed to showcase the connection of Greece's rich tradition, history, and popular culture with the country's modern generation.

In the Grand Final on May 11, 2024, Marina Satti finished in 11th place for Greece with 126 points.
